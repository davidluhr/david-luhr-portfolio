About David Luhr

It’s possible to drive serious business results without killing the fun.

I love creating delightful and engaging websites that add value for users and facilitate better business. I enjoy taking on complex problems, improving processes, and measuring impact, all without being a grump about it.

As someone with way too many interests and hobbies, I’ve had an unconventional trajectory in the worlds of design and development.

I’ve gone from contemporary art museums, doing exhibition design and fabrication, to for-profit ecommerce, implementing A/B testing, conversion rate optimization, responsive HTML email marketing, and web design to drive sales, to higher education, pushing traditional boundaries with crafted, audience-specific experiences.

I’m currently the Senior Web Designer at Westminster College, in Salt Lake City, Utah. I oversee Westminster’s user experience, visual design, front-end development, and a slew of other responsibilities. I’m passionate about innovating Westminster’s online presence and breaking higher education convention to speak more genuinely to students about the school’s experience and values.

Check out my work

Outside of my professional work, I’m constantly creating, exploring, and learning. On any given weekend, I may spend my time riding my BMX bike at the local skateparks, whittling a spoon at my traditional woodworking bench, or hiking through the trees in the local mountains. I try to travel as frequently as possible, and fell in love with Japan as my favorite destination. When I catch a spare moment, I obsessively read sci-fi and personal finance books, or I devour e-courses, webinars, and Medium articles that expand my technology stack and design thinking.

All in all, I am addicted to the steep learning curves of taking on something new, and there are simply too many fascinating, fun, and challenging new ventures to ever settle for being a specialist.

About This Site

This site was hand-crafted using Atom, the GitHub repo is live on my personal account, and I'm hosting this Jekyll-powered static site on GitHub pages.

The design process followed my normal progression from rapid sketch iterations to digital mockups before diving into code.

For this project, I used the beta release Adobe Xd for the digital mockups and early prototyping. I've fallen in love with this workflow and look forward to using it much more.

In the code, I use SASS and a custom Gulp.JS setup that I modified from ShakyShane's popular repo so I could use my favorite development tools.
